Every
7 seconds,
someone dies
from pollution.

Not in a movie. Right now. As you read this. A child in Delhi. A farmer in Bihar. An elderly woman in Chennai — breathing air that has quietly turned against them.

India generates 9.4 million tonnes of e-waste alone every year. Most of it rots in open landfills, leaching mercury, lead, and cadmium into the soil and water that feeds us.

And the worst part? It was all preventable. If someone had simply picked it up.

⚠ Air Pollution Deaths in India — This Year 1,230,000+
